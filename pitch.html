01 · The Story

Every morning,
Dakar bleeds time.

Dakar is a city of 4 million people squeezed onto a 550 km² peninsula. Twice a day, it grinds to a halt. Commuters spend hours in gridlock, miss jobs, lose income — and the country loses $1.4 billion per year to traffic congestion alone.


The apps that could help? They're in French. They assume literacy. They assume smartphones. They don't speak Wolof. They weren't built for the 44% of Dakarois who walk because nothing else makes sense — or the millions more who navigate the city by instinct, community knowledge, and word of mouth.


JAKKAL was born from a simple, urgent question: What if the city could talk to you — in your language?

04 · AI & Accessibility

Built for everyone.
Not just the connected few.

46% of Senegalese adults are not literate in French. Existing apps exclude them entirely. JAKKAL is built from the ground up with a "voice-first, text-second" philosophy.


🎙️
Wolof ASR Engine
Powered by open-source Wolof speech recognition (Baamtu Datamation + Google WAXAL datasets). Users speak naturally — the app understands accents, slang, and place names.
🖼️
Icon-Only Navigation Mode
A full icon-driven UI with no text required. Tap a bus emoji to find buses. Tap a car to find a cab. Universally understood visual language.
📱
JAKKAL Lite (USSD + SMS)
For users without smartphones: text *707# to get a Wolof SMS with traffic status and next bus time. Works on any phone, any carrier.
🧠
AI Traffic Prediction
Machine learning model trained on Dakar traffic patterns, prayer times, market days, and school hours. Predicts congestion before it happens.
🌍
Community Crowdsource Layer
Users report accidents, roadblocks, floods, broken traffic lights. Each report earns mobile data credits — turning commuters into contributors.
Global Benchmarks We're Inspired By
🇧🇷 Moovit (Brazil/Global)
Multimodal public transit. 1B+ trips planned. We adapt its multimodal logic for informal Dakar transport.
🇮🇱 Waze (Crowdsourced Traffic)
Community-reported incidents. We bring this model to Dakar with Wolof voice alerts.
🇮🇳 Ola / Namma Yatri (India)
Ride-hail built for informal markets, vernacular languages, and low connectivity.
🇰🇪 Little Cab (Kenya)
Africa-first ride-hail that works offline and integrates M-PESA. Model for our Wave mobile payment integration.
🇸🇳 Wave (Senegal — proof of concept)
Took 50% of fintech market in 3 years in Senegal by being truly local. We follow the same playbook for mobility.

05 · Brand Identity

A name rooted
in the land.

The Name
JAKKAL
Jakkal means "the road" or "the path" in Wolof.
It conveys journey, direction, and forward movement.
It's pronounceable in every Senegalese language.
It's ownable, memorable, and unmistakably local.
The Tagline
"Xam sa jakkal." — Know your road.
In Wolof, then French: "Connaître son chemin."
The Logo Concept
A stylized letter J that doubles as a road curving forward on a map — with a location pin forming the dot. Thick, confident, golden. Works at any size from app icon to billboard.
